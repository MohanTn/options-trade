🤖 At a Glance — Do We Agree on the Goal?

My understanding of what you want

ThetaDesk already archives one permanent Postgres row per trading day (VegaFlowDailySnapshots, since migration 20260702205705) holding the full intraday series of CE and PE Σ-vega %-change vs the morning open — for both the near-week and near-month expiries — sampled every ≥45s during market hours, alongside NIFTY spot at each tick. That is almost certainly the "post-crest database" referenced: a curve that typically rises to an intraday peak (the crest) as one side gets bought, then decays back down. Today this series is consumed live and same-day only: ChainAnalysisService.Compose() folds the current day's vega-flow reading into a weighted BiasScore alongside PCR, OI flow, max pain, skew and wall migration, and SignalEngine uses that score to skew short-leg target deltas by up to ±30%. The only other "pattern" logic that exists is a live, rule-based opening-range breakout in the frontend (vegaFlowRange.ts) — not a backtested, cross-day statistical study. The ask is different: look across all archived days (currently on the order of a week and a half's worth) for a repeatable shape in the post-crest decay that predicts something useful for a seller — direction, timing, or confirmation — and decide how a validated finding would feed back into the existing pipeline. This document scopes that analysis: what data it reads, what "crest" and "decay" mean, what outcome variable counts as an edge, and where the result would plug in (a new Compose() driver, an advisory-only note, or a new SignalEngine timing gate) — without yet running the analysis or changing any scoring code, since this session has no live connection to the operator's running Postgres instance.

1 · The Concept, In My Own Words

Every trading day, ThetaDesk already watches the NIFTY option chain reprice in real time and tracks, side by side, how much the calls' total vega and the puts' total vega have moved away from that morning's opening reading. On most days that curve isn't flat — one side (or both) gets bid up through the morning, peaks somewhere around midday (the crest), and then bleeds back down into the close as the buying pressure that drove it fades. That shape is already captured and permanently archived, one row per day, going back to the day this feature shipped.

The idea here is that the shape of that post-crest decay — how fast it falls, how far, on which side, relative to where NIFTY spot was sitting at the time — might not be random. If calls decaying hard after a crest tends to precede a specific kind of afternoon (say, a fade back toward the day's open, or a confirmation that the morning's move is exhausted), that's a genuine timing or directional edge a premium seller can lean on: a reason to enter (or wait to enter) a short strangle with more conviction than the chain's static Greeks alone would give. This matters because the fund's whole edge is selling theta — anything that improves the odds of selling into range-bound, decaying-vol conditions (rather than into a move that's about to run) directly improves win rate and reduces the frequency of adjustment/stop-loss events.

The catch, made explicit in this document, is that the archive is young — on the order of a week and a half of trading days as of today — so the honest first step is to define the methodology precisely (what counts as a crest, what counts as decay, what forward outcome proves an edge) rather than to declare a pattern found on too few days.

2 · What the Code Does Today

The data already exists and is already used — just narrowly, and only same-day. ChainAnalysisService.AnalyzeAsync (run on every chain scan, and re-triggered every 60s during market hours by ChainAnalysisRefresher) computes each expiry's Σ-vega basket and its %-change versus that day's morning capture, appends a sample to a Redis list (chain:vegaflow:{yyyyMMdd}, ≥45s apart, market hours only), and — once 15:30 IST is reached — archives that day's full series permanently into Postgres as one VegaFlowDailySnapshot row (idempotent via a unique index on TradingDate). The same reading also flows, live, into Compose(), which blends it (0.6 near-week / 0.4 near-month) with PCR, OI order-flow, max-pain pull, IV skew and OI-wall migration into a single BiasScore ∈ [−1, +1]. SignalEngine.GenerateCandidatesAsync reads that score and skews short-leg target deltas toward the safer side by up to ±30%. On the frontend, ChainAnalysisView.tsx plots the intraday series, and vegaFlowRange.ts implements the one piece of 'pattern' logic that exists today: an opening-range breakout rule (first-N-minutes' high/low band; a later close outside it fires a sound alert) — a live, same-day heuristic, not something derived from historical data.

flowchart TD
  A[ChainAnalysisService.AnalyzeAsync] --> B[Compose per-expiry CE/PE vega change %]
  B --> C[Redis chain:vegaflow:yyyyMMdd list]
  C -->|>=15:30 IST, once/day| D[(Postgres VegaFlowDailySnapshots)]
  B --> E[Compose BiasScore]
  E --> F[SignalEngine +/-30% delta skew]
  C --> G[GET /api/v1/analysis/vega-flow]
  G --> H[ChainAnalysisView.tsx chart]
  H --> I[vegaFlowRange.ts opening-range breakout alert]
  D -.one day at a time.-> J[GetVegaFlowSnapshotAsync history picker]

Nothing today reads across multiple archived days at once — D is written to and read from one day at a time; there is no aggregate query, no crest/decay feature extraction, and no correlation against any forward outcome.

10 · Implementation Sketch (Non-Binding)

Non-binding — for orientation only.

  1. Add a GetAllVegaFlowSnapshotsAsync()-style query (or a standalone script against ThetaDeskDbContext) that loads every archived VegaFlowDailySnapshot, deserialising PointsJson per day.
  2. Per day, per tenor (week/month), per side (CE/PE): smooth the series and locate the crest (time, height) and the subsequent decay (depth, slope, duration into close).
  3. Join each day's decay features against the chosen forward outcome (OQ1) — e.g. next trading day's open-to-close NIFTY return.
  4. Bucket days by decay-shape features and compute hit-rate / average forward move per bucket, alongside the raw sample count per bucket.
  5. Report the findings — numbers and caveats, not a code change — for the operator to review before any follow-up arch doc proposes wiring a validated finding into Compose(), an advisory field, or SignalEngine.
