1 · The Concept, In My Own Words

The idea: the unrealized P&L shown alongside the vega-flow chart is the single number that tells you, mid-session, whether the seller's book is working — yet it currently renders as 0.78rem muted-slate caption text, visually identical to the chart's explanatory subtitle. You want it to behave like a P&L readout: bold, green in profit / red in loss, and bigger at 0.95rem, so a glance answers 'am I up or down?' without hunting for a small grey number.

The problem avoided: misreading or overlooking the desk's live P&L while watching vega flow, which is exactly the moment (an opening-range breach, a bias shift) when that number matters most.

2 · What the Code Does Today

On the Option Chain page, ChainAnalysisView fetches the portfolio Greeks every 60s and passes portfolioGreeks?.unrealisedPnl into VegaFlowSection (ChainAnalysisView.tsx:88-95, 146). That section renders the chart in one of two shells:

  • Normal (in-page card): a SectionLabel ('Vega flow — Δ Σν vs morning…'), the vega-read note, a ⛶ Maximize button, and the three-lane SVG chart. The P&L is not shown here (will be added per T1).
  • Maximized (modal): the same chart inside Modal, whose subtitle is a string: 'Δ Σν vs morning, near week · Unrealized P&L: ₹12,340' (ChainAnalysisView.tsx:233-235). Modal.tsx:60 renders that subtitle at 0.78rem in color.textSub (muted slate) — no bolding, no profit/loss color.

The theme already ships pnlColor(n) → n ≥ 0 ? '#16a34a' : '#dc2626' (theme.ts:79), used by the four Σ-change rows in the expiry panels, and the chart's series palette deliberately reserves green/red for P&L semantics (comment at ChainAnalysisView.tsx:173).
